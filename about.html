Extracurriculars

Stanford Archery

I shoot Barebow on Stanford's collegiate archery team! The team is comprised of Stanford undergraduates and graduate students under the direction of former U.S. national team member Francis Parchaso. Stanford Archery is a recreational club sport with a mission to do its competitive best, bring safe archery to the community, and to have fun. I currently serve as the Recruiting Director and enjoy talking about the club and onboarding new recruits! I didn't shoot before coming to Stanford, but learning has been very fun, and the community is really relaxed. We shoot every Sunday on the Stanford Range and have member practices every single weekday. Although I'm still a terrible shot, I enjoy cracking bad archery puns, grabbing meals after practice, and gawking over the accuracy of some of the deadshots on the team.

Stanford Dragonboat

Dragonboating is an exciting water sport with historical roots in Chinese folklore in which 22-person boats compete in races ranging 200m to 8km. Stanford Dragonboat is a recreational team affiliated with the California Dragonboat Association (CDBA), and we compete year-round in races around the West Coast against schools such as UC Berkeley, Cal State schools, and San Francisco University, among other schools. I was first introduced to Dragonboating by my dad as the local Chinese church participated in Lake Lanier rowing competitions, complete with exciting festivals packed with observers, street food, and Chinese crafts. The Dragonboating commununity at Stanford is very friendly, and rowing is a great way to destress after a stacked week of classes. On Saturday mornings, we wake up and drive in Zipcars to a local waterway where we paddle and splash water all over the person in front of us to the rhythm of shouts or a drum. After practice, we ALWAYS make sure to grab food, be it In-and-Out, Pho, Hotpot, Dumplings, or Boba. It's a great way to spend Saturdays--putting on calories from eating with friends after burning them on a workout--and I love that I get a chance to get off-campus!

Common Origins

Common Origins is Stanford's largest non-audition dance group. I joined CO in Winter Quarter after showing up to one of their workshops recommended by a friend, and I really enjoyed the energy and enthusiasm of the group. Dancing was new to me, but CO is extremely new dancer friendly, and the COre team really works hard to include everyone in their hip-hop style. Each quarter, there's a set of choreographers that take the lead for individual pieces, andfor performances, dancers can pick to dance in as many pieces as they want. Thus, the pieces range in different styles based on the choreographer, which is really nice. I danced to choreo to artists ranging from NIKI to Halsey to Joyner Lucas. I really love the support, warmth, and energy of CO (best described as wholesome), and it really comes out in workshops, practices, and dance parties (which are SO much fun and way better than normal parties--if you haven't been to a dancer party, you haven't lived) Common Origins performs at events such as Breaking Ground (the first large dance event that historically CO organizes), EnCounter Culture (ECC), and Family Weekend, and it's quite exhilirating to dance in front of hundreds of people alongside really supportive team members.
